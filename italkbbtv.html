iTalkBB TV

Background

iTalkBB TV was launched as a video streaming platform in July 2019. Its goal is to provide high-quality video resources for audiences interested in Chinese related videos, such as Chinese drama, Chinese movies, and trending videos in the North American Chinese community.

My contribution

I was invited to evaluate and improve the design of its key pages, especially the homepage with the product manager, from the perspective of user experience, in order to enhance the watching experience.

Problem

As a website that has only been launched for a year, the design of the previous version was not optimized for its targeted user group. Moreover, the design also had some issues affecting user experience such as consistency and logic errors. The parts that needed to be modified are marked with flashing red.

Old version of homepage

Menu

After sorting out the data, I found that the majority of users are Chinese speakers, and more than half of them are non-resident alien, such as Chinese students and short term employees. This means the targeted users are inclined to Asian style of user interface. Asian users usually have a stronger purpose when using products, therefore the search function is particularly important. In the new design, the input box is added to the menu, which can be used as a first-level entrance for users to use the product. Additionally, placeholder is used effectively to display popular searches.

According to feedback, although some American-Chinese users are proficient in Chinese listening and speaking, they have difficulty reading Chinese characters, so the language switch option is added to the menu.

What's more, the menu shows which page the user is currently viewing. For example, ‘Home’ will be highlighted when the user is on homepage.

Login

In the old design, buttons with the same function have different forms, which caused confusion to users. For example, the login button appears in two shapes. In the new design, one of the login buttons is removed to maintain consistency.

Previous version of login

In order to increase the activation rate, users need to be clearly shown the benefits they can enjoy after registration. My idea is to show directly what the exclusive benefits are for signup users, such as watching records, offline video and notification, to increase the attractiveness to unregistered users.

Modified login

When the cursor hovers over the Login button, the Login window pops up and the background fades.

Login window

Lists

An icon is added before the title for easy identification. As shown, a movie projector-style icon before the movie title.

Clickable texts, in this case the movie tags, have been transformed into button-looking to better distinguish them from other texts.

In addition, in my previous investigation, I found that the confusing logic of buttons brought unnecessary thinking burden to users. Second, users do not understand the role of the 'Other' tag as the designer originally intended. In the designer’s imagination, the ‘Other’ tag works as a language related movie tag pointing to a page containing movies in languages other than Chinese and Cantonese, but in fact users often think it refers to movie tags such as action movies or science fiction movies. Moreover, in the a/b test where 'More' tag are eliminated, users consider it works the same as ‘All’ and ‘More’ in functionality.
