iCare (School project)

Introductions

The rise of mobile technology has brought an influx of mobile apps for seniors in recent years. As of 2016, research showed carried out by Pew Research Center that 42% of all senior citizens owned a smartphone in the United States. On the one hand, there is no doubt that senior citizens are making their mark in the world of technology. On the other hand, seniors are not usually considered the target user of high-tech products. Although many apps have been designed for any age group, seniors might still face unique challenges when using these apps.

iCare app aims to be an elderly-friendly app and give users helpful tools for making every day healthy life easier. The app includes not only tools for exercise and steps, but a tracker for everyday diet. Everything section about the app is personalized, making it easy for users to set goals and work on positive lifestyle changes. Moreover, users can also connect and sync wearable devices, add friends, and join supportive online health community circles.

Key Features

  • Visual elements to help to recall
  • Different color blocks to differentiate contents
  • Large enough fonts to increase legibility
  • More pickers and fewer input boxes for easier operation
  • Dedicated navigation to show important elements upfront
  • Help texts and tooltips to guide senior users
