DC Metro Ticket Machine Design

My role

I worked with two other teammates to design the new DC Metro ticket machine interface. As the leader of the team, I was mainly responsible for user interface design, user testing and prototyping in the project.

Problem

The outdated ticket machine, as the only way for passengers to purchase tickets, passes and metro cards, does not provide a user-friendly experience. At peak times and holiday seasons, a passenger could wait up to 8 minutes for his/her turn to use the ticket machine.

Based on our observation and interviews，the following factors that lead to difficulties have been summed up.

DC Metro ticket machine

1. Terrible human-machine interaction design.
  • Non-touchable character LCD that only shows some basic information.
  • Only three operable buttons are provided for user to complete all operations.
  • No ‘Go back’ option when making mistakes.
2. Inexplicit ticketing process logic.
  • The ticketing process mandates that all users have to purchase a metro card in the first place and then load the desired ticket value into the metro card. However, users wouldn’t be informed that no single-use tickets are available.
3. Lack of guidance and functions.
  • Users who want to buy tickets or passes are often confused since there are no instructions to guide them.
  • There are no buttons assigned for calling out the reload page, instead, users are required to swipe their cards.
  • There is no back button.

Goal

To build a user interface that meets the user's psychological expectations, so that users can quickly complete the purchase process without additional guidance.

To build the brand identity that helps to increase brand awareness.

Scope

All users who need to purchase metro tickets, passes and metro cards. The main user group is between the ages of 15 and 60 years old, and some of them are technologically challenged.

From interview to persona

Interviews helped our team to extract important information from users’ feedback on the use of ticket machines. We integrated all the information into personas and listed the pain points.

Design principles

After our research and user interviews, we have come up with a set of design principles to admit while designing. Summarized into three words, they are Simple, Useful, and Desirable.

Ideation

Existing interaction logic often confuses users so much that they cannot even complete tasks within a reasonable time without help.

User testing

Applying the method of agile development and lean UX, we performed two user testing on the minimum viable product and finally formed a redesigned prototype after two iterations.

Redesign

1. The homepage design corresponds to user expectations.

  • Multi-language options are added for user convenience.
  • ‘Quick Booking’ lists metro stations near popular activities and attractions. For users heading to these destinations, one-step selection shortens ticket purchase time.

Evaluation

1. Save time buying tickets by 75%-91%.

Users used to take approximately 4 minutes to complete the ticket purchase task. With the new designs, it is reduced to approximately 1 minute. In particular, 'Quick Booking' greatly reduces the time to approximately 20 seconds.

2. Save time reloading metro card by 37.5%.

Users used to take approximately 40 seconds to complete the reload task. With the new designs, it is reduced to approximately 25 seconds.

3. Increased task success rate of purchasing ticket without seeking assistance up to 100%.

In the survey, 30 % of users (50% of novice users) sought help from staff or other passengers in order to complete the purchase. With the new designs, all users can successfully complete the purchase task independently.

Final thoughts

In this project, we practiced what we learned about user experience. The positive feedback we gathered from users on the new DC Metro ticket machine design has also fully proved that good user-centered design can greatly improve user satisfaction.



Part 2

DC Metro Reload and Tracking App

This is an independent personal project derived from the previous project. The app contains the most desirable features, including a real-time bus tracking system and an online metro card reload system. It is dedicated to providing a whole new mobility enhancement for users.

Flow chart

Passengers, especially frequent riders, can quickly complete the reload operation on their mobile phones to avoid waiting in line. Additionally, the app provides real-time updates of all of the buses and metro operating in DC area. Passengers get easy access to arrival times of their favorite stops as well as alerts and delays.

After usability testing, the parts that did not meet user expectations have been redesigned. The timetable is changed to be displayed in graphs instead of charts, reducing the user's thinking process. In addition, 'share with friends' is added as a way to acquire more new users.
