DC Metro Ticket Machine Design

My role

I worked with my teammates to design the new DC Metro ticket machine interface. As the leader of the team, I was mainly responsible for ideation, user testing, user interface design and prototyping in the project.

Problem

The outdated ticket machine, as the only way for passengers to purchase tickets, passes and metro cards, does not provide a user-friendly experience. At peak times and holiday seasons, a passenger could wait up to 8 minutes for his/her turn to use the ticket machine.

DC Metro ticket machine

Based on our observation and interviews, the following factors that lead to difficulties have been summed up.

Analysis

1. Terrible hardware and user interface design.
  • Non-touchable character LCD that only shows some basic information.
  • Only three operable buttons are provided for user to complete all operations.
  • No ‘Go back’ option when making mistakes.
2. Inexplicit ticketing process logic.
  • All users are required to purchase a metro card in the first place and then load the desired ticket value into the metro card. However, users wouldn’t be informed that no single-use tickets are available.
3. Lack of guidance and functionalities.
  • Users who want to buy tickets or passes are often confused since there are no instructions to guide them.
  • There are no buttons assigned for calling out the reload page, instead, users are required to swipe their cards.
  • Complex and non-automated fare calculation.

Goal

To build a user interface that meets the user's psychological expectations, so that users can quickly complete the purchase process without additional guidance.

To build the brand identity that helps to increase brand awareness.

Scope

All users who need to purchase metro tickets, passes and metro cards. The main user group is between the ages of 15 and 60 years old, and some of them are technologically challenged.

From interview to persona

Interviews helped our team to extract important information from users’ feedback on the use of ticket machines. We integrated all the information into personas and listed the pain points.

Design principles

After our research and user interviews, we have come up with a set of design principles to admit while designing. Summarized into three words, they are Simple, Useful, and Desirable.

Usability testing

The usability test we designed includes two parts: observation and interview. The users used the prototype to complete the three specified tasks and answered the relevant questions. Through the usability test, we have summarized the following pain points.

  • The homepage has a large amount of information and the classification is not clear.
  • 80% of travelers use the metro to go to the most popular tourist destinations, such as the White House, Congress or the Cherry Blossom Festival. They know the metro station of their destination.
  • Users have a great demand for round-trip tickets since they don't want to buy tickets at metro stations near crowded tourist attractions.
  • There are many metro lines, it is very laborious to find metro station on the map.
  • The user interface design needs to be improved, such as the go back button and the language selection.
  • The payment process confuses some users. The user cannot see the amount inserted and here is no guidance for payment.

Therefore, we have improved the user flow and added a new quick booking flow.

Modified user flow

Second version

Compared to the initial design, my team has improved the user flow. We fixed the issues and discovered that adding a shortcut channel on the homepage to divert users can help some users with clear goals complete the ticket purchase process more quickly. At the same time, another map option has been added to meet the needs of users.

However, in the second version usability test, we still found some problems.

  • Non-native English speakers find it unfriendly to use the search box.
  • The presentation of the two amounts on the payment page is similar, which increases the user’s cognitive memory load.

Finaly prototype

We adjusted the visual design of the interface and optimized details. For example, eliminating the "Next" button to allow the entire product to automate the process, adding payment instructions, etc.

Evaluation

Saved time buying tickets by 81%-95%

Users used to take an average of 5.4 minutes to complete the ticket purchase task. With the new designs, it is reduced to approximately 1 minute. In particular, 'Quick Booking' greatly reduces the time to approximately 0.25 minutes.

Saved time reloading metro card by 64%

Users used to take approximately 1.14 minutes to complete the reload task. With the new designs, it is reduced to approximately 0.40 minutes.

Increased task success rate of purchasing ticket without seeking assistance up to 100%

In the survey, 50% of new users sought help from staff or other passengers in order to complete the purchase. With the new designs, all users can successfully complete the purchase task independently.

Final thoughts

In this project, we practiced what we learned about user experience. The positive feedback we gathered from users on the new DC Metro ticket machine design has also fully proved that good user-centered design can greatly improve user satisfaction.

Metro Reload and Tracking App

Product definition

Metro stations are overcrowded during rush hours and the old ticket machines make the situation worse. Passengers, especially frequent riders, want to reload metro card as quickly as possible. It is an excellent idea to integrate the reload function into the mobile app. Therefore the purpose of this product is to allow daily commuters to skip the line and reload their metro card faster.

Research

This is a product from 0 to 1, so I first conducted user interviews to understand the real needs of users.

Analysis

Personas

Brian, a 25-year-old company employee working in dc, uses the metro to commute between home and office every day. On weekends, he uses bus and metro with his friends to go to park hiking there. Occasionally, he would use the long vacation to visit friends and family around the city.

Ideation and MVP

The original app design only had the function of reloading the metro card. As a MVP, it confirmed that this app does solve the user's problem, that is, quickly complete the task of reloading metro card.

MVP prototype

The disadvantage is that the only one function of the app is not enough to support all users, such as users who do not yet have a metro card. In addition, functional deficiencies will reduce the number of times users use apps, so that they gradually forget the existence of apps, which reduces user retention.

SWOT map

Metrics

  • Time occupied

    • Users can complete the reload task within the prescribed time. In this case, the task of reload $50 within the 30 seconds. Second, the task of finding the red line metro schedule at Medical Center station within 40 seconds.

  • Desirability

    • The user's rating of the app in the System Usability Scale should be higher than the grade B, that is, the average score is higher than 68.

Final design

Final design

Aquisition - To get more users

In addition to reload, users can now purchase metro cards in the app. This group of users who have metro travel needs but do not have a metro card may choose to buy a metro card through the app.

Retention - To get users back

The app provides real-time updates of all of the buses and metro operating in DC area. Passengers get easy access to arrival times of their favorite stops as well as alerts and delays. This feature greatly increases the user's usage rate.

Referral - To get users to invite more users

Social attributes have been added, ‘share with friends’ function allows users to share their carbon footprint with friends, which helps spread the app.

Interactions

Animation efects have become an important part of improving user experience in the past two years because it can bring continuity to users.

Outcome

In the usability test, six testers completed the tasks of reloading and querying the metro bus schedule within the specified time. The average SUS score of the final design is 87.5, a satisfactory level.
